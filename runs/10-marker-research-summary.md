## High-Priority MO Gaps (per marker)


### Apolipoprotein B

- No MO optimal target exists for any population stratum. The SM anchor provides population reference intervals (80–110 mg/dL for all adults) but no practitioner-defined metabolic optimization thresholds are documented.
- No sex-specific MO targets. Male and female strata (18–50, 51–65, 66–79, 80+) each have SM reference intervals but no corresponding MO thresholds are known for either sex.
- No age-stratified MO targets. The SM anchor covers 4 age bands per sex but no MO practitioner guidance differentiates targets by age decade or life stage.

### Fasting Insulin

- No MO optimization target exists for any stratum. SM upper limits range from 20.6 to 24.4 μIU/mL across strata, but practitioners in the metabolic optimization space frequently argue these population-based norms reflect an insulin-resistant population and that true optimal levels are substantially lower.
- No sex-specific MO targets identified. Female strata have lower SM upper limits (20.6–24.4) than male strata (23.1–24.4), but it is unclear whether MO practitioners apply different targets by sex.

### Hba1C

- No MO-optimal HbA1c target exists for male_18_50 stratum (SM range 4.6–7.4%). Practitioner-derived optimal targets for young healthy males are unknown from this anchor.
- No MO-optimal HbA1c target exists for female_18_50 stratum (SM range 4.6–6.8%). Sex-specific metabolic optimization guidance for young women is absent.
- No MO guidance for male_51_65 (SM range 4.8–9.8%) or female_51_65 (SM range 4.9–8.9%). The SM ranges extend deep into diabetic territory without any annotation about whether MO would reject these upper bounds.

### Lipoprotein(a)

- No sex-stratified MO targets exist — Lp(a) levels differ by sex (higher in post-menopausal females), but the SM anchor covers only 'all' sex with no male/female breakdown.
- No ethnicity/ancestry-stratified MO targets — Lp(a) distributions vary substantially by genetic ancestry (e.g., higher median in African ancestry populations), yet the SM anchor has no such stratification.
- No known MO practitioner targets (Attia, Mason, etc.) are cited or visible — the ≤75 nmol/L 'optimal' label is an SM anchor status with no attributed MO source.

### TG/HDL ratio

- No MO-optimal TG/HDL ratio threshold is exposed for any population stratum. The anchor confirms MO targets exist internally but are hidden from the agent.
- Population-specific MO targets for African Americans are flagged as unreliable per Sumner 2005 (PMID 15983289) but no alternative MO marker or adjusted threshold is documented.

### 17 Hydroxyprogesterone

- No known practitioner-defined optimal targets for 17-OHP in metabolic optimization context
- Sex-specific optimal ranges unclear despite SM stratification
- Unclear role of 17-OHP in metabolic optimization beyond CAH screening

### Absolute Lymphocytes

- No sex-stratified MO targets exist — the SM anchor provides only a single all_adults stratum (all sexes, age 18+). Practitioners may recommend different optimal lymphocyte ranges for males vs. females given known sex differences in immune function.
- No age-stratified MO targets exist — immune senescence causes lymphocyte counts to decline with age, yet the SM anchor lumps all adults 18+ into one range. Practitioners focused on longevity likely have age-specific guidance.
- No practitioner-derived optimal range for absolute lymphocytes is documented. Key metabolic/longevity practitioners (Attia, Patrick, Longo) discuss immune biomarkers but no specific absolute lymphocyte targets are referenced in this anchor.

### Absolute Neutrophils

- No sex-stratified MO targets exist — the SM anchor covers all_adults only, but neutrophil counts differ by sex and practitioners may have sex-specific optimal bands
- No age-stratified MO targets — elderly immune remodeling (immunosenescence) may shift optimal ANC, but no practitioner guidance exists for 50+ or 65+ populations

### Albumin

- No practitioner-specific optimal albumin targets for longevity or healthspan identified across any stratum

### Alpha-1-Acid Glycoprotein

- No known MO practitioner target for AAGP in any population stratum
- No MO guidance for female_18_50 despite SM range (40–100 mg/dL) being substantially lower than older women (80–200 mg/dL)
- No MO position on the inflammation-exclusion threshold of 100 mg/dL (1 g/L) as a health optimization boundary

## High-Priority RC Gaps (per marker)


### Apolipoprotein B

- PMC10498001 is cited for the all_adults SM range but its specific findings relevant to optimal (not population-normal) ApoB levels are undocumented.
- PMID 23584084 and PMID 33736827 are listed in known_research_context but their specific contributions to RC evidence are not annotated.

### Fasting Insulin

- 14 PMIDs listed but none characterized. It is unknown which contain prospective outcome data linking fasting insulin levels to disease endpoints (T2D, CVD, metabolic syndrome, all-cause mortality).
- No research consensus identified for sex- or age-stratified risk thresholds. Even if overall population-level prognostic data exists in the listed PMIDs, stratified evidence (e.g., what fasting insulin level predicts T2D in females 51–65 specifically) may be absent.

### Hba1C

- No continuous risk-curve evidence (J-curve or U-curve) linking HbA1c to all-cause mortality or cardiovascular events in non-diabetic populations is documented in this anchor.
- No stratified RC evidence by sex or age group. The SM anchor is stratified but the known_research_context provides no indication of which papers support age- or sex-specific targets.
- No annotation of landmark trial results (ACCORD, ADVANCE, VADT, UKPDS) within the known_research_context. Multiple 2008-era PMIDs (18539916, 18539917, 18540046) likely correspond to these trials but are not annotated.

### Lipoprotein(a)

- No PMIDs, PMCIDs, or DOIs are listed — the SM anchor has zero cited research, making it impossible to trace the evidence for the 75 and 125 nmol/L thresholds.
- No research consensus on whether 75 nmol/L or 125 nmol/L corresponds to clinically meaningful cardiovascular event risk inflection points.
- No RC evidence on Lp(a) lowering and hard cardiovascular outcomes — emerging trials (e.g., HORIZON/pelacarsen) are not referenced.

### TG/HDL ratio

- No RC consensus statement on TG/HDL ratio as a cardiometabolic risk predictor is referenced (e.g., no AHA/ACC or ESC guideline citation).
- The implied SM ratio range derivable from upstream anchors (triglycerides SM range / HDL-cholesterol SM range) is not computed or stated, making it impossible to compare SM-implied vs. RC-consensus ranges.

### 17 Hydroxyprogesterone

- PMID 28582566 and 33527139 relevance to metabolic outcomes unclear
- No research consensus on 17-OHP association with insulin resistance or metabolic syndrome

### Absolute Lymphocytes

- Only one paper (PMC7817015) is cited and it appears to be a reference interval source, not an outcomes study. No research consensus exists linking specific absolute lymphocyte levels to longevity, all-cause mortality, or metabolic disease risk.
- No sex-stratified or age-stratified outcome studies are referenced. Research consensus on what lymphocyte count predicts morbidity/mortality in specific demographics is absent from this anchor.

### Absolute Neutrophils

- Only one source (PMC7817015, Ghana-based) underpins the SM range — no Western-population RC evidence is cited for the 0.94–3.52 x10^9/L range
- No outcome-linked RC studies — no evidence connecting specific ANC levels within the normal range to morbidity, mortality, or disease risk

### Albumin

- No systematic review or meta-analysis evidence linking specific albumin thresholds to all-cause mortality in healthy populations

### Alpha-1-Acid Glycoprotein

- No prospective evidence linking AAGP levels within SM-normal range to hard cardiovascular or mortality outcomes
- No consensus on AAGP threshold for metabolic risk stratification independent of CRP
