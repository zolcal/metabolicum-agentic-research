# Executive summary

The Metabolic Optimization research pipeline is built around two core risks. First, the gate between an agent producing output and that output entering Supabase must be strict enough that unsupported claims cannot be laundered into the database. Second, source discovery must use legitimate platform-native or source-native access so agents read what practitioners actually said rather than guessing from general model memory. The design addresses both risks through five structural commitments. The pipeline runs per wave — a batch of markers — triggered by generated research briefs (section 19): each brief is a search seed carrying source pointers plus the marker's stripped SM reference rows, which are withheld from discovery and extraction and revealed only to the council.

The first commitment is that every claim must conform to the BiomarkerClaim contract before persistence. The verbatim quote field is mandatory, the source URL is mandatory, the retrieval timestamp is mandatory, the section-fifteen evidence sub-grade is mandatory, and the paradigm attribution (one of SM, RC, or MO — PM is a future paradigm not produced by this pipeline) is mandatory. Anti-hallucination becomes a property of the data model, not a property of agent good behavior.

The second commitment is that ingestion is source-first and assembly is marker-driven. A source — a podcast episode, a YouTube video, a blog post — is fetched, transcribed, and extracted once. The claims it produces are tagged with all applicable markers through a many-to-many link table. Per-marker SQL artifacts are then assembled from the claim store on demand. This eliminates the wasted-evidence failure mode where a ninety-minute Attia podcast covering five markers gets re-processed five times.

The third commitment is that source discovery uses legitimate platform access, one channel at a time. Basic research uses YouTube, podcasts, and public web/practitioner surfaces; X/Twitter, Telegram, and LinkedIn belong to the content-discovery maintenance phase (section 3). X/Twitter is retained for maintenance and content discovery, not as a primary evidence-bearing source; the first implementation uses `twitter-mcp` plus SearXNG-discovered public links, with Grok or another X-native sanctioned path reserved for future endpoint expansion. Gemini 2.5 and later handles YouTube through its native URL ingestion. Podcasts use verified RSS feeds first, with Podscan, Listen Notes, and transcription providers only as augmentation or fallback for discovery and transcription gaps. Telegram is restricted to the public `t.me/s/` preview surface because Telegram's Bot Developer Terms explicitly prohibit dataset construction through the Bot API (telegram.org/tos/bot-developers, retrieved 2026-05-13). LinkedIn is restricted to manual seeds because the hiQ Labs versus LinkedIn litigation confirmed contract-liability risk even where public-profile scraping arguments survive under CFAA analysis. Facebook and Instagram are deferred entirely because Meta Content Library requires academic affiliation, special review, and a controlled research environment.

The fourth commitment is that validation follows a council-of-agents pattern and fails into quarantine. The extractor agent, the reviewer agent, and the decider agent come from three different model families, so a hallucination must survive three independent checks across different architectures. Du, Li, Torralba, Tenenbaum, and Mordatch (2023), "Improving Factuality and Reasoning in Language Models through Multiagent Debate" (arXiv:2305.14325, retrieved 2026-05-13), state that "our approach improves the factual validity of generated content, reducing fallacious answers and hallucinations that contemporary models are prone to." A Standard Medical anchor table provides sanity-check ranges per marker so that wildly out-of-bound MO claims surface as paradigm-divergence flags rather than entering the database silently.

The fifth commitment is that research target envelopes can guide research without becoming evidence. For basic research the operational envelope is the brief's stripped SM rows used as a soft alignment reference; an optional private envelope-fact layer exists for non-publishable internal goals. Either way the numbers are withheld from discovery and extraction and revealed only to the council, which uses them to measure convergence and flag contradictions — never to approve, score, or coerce a value. Envelope facts are never publishable, never scored as evidence (`evidence_weight: 0`), never exported to `metasync`, and never cited as support for a claim. They preserve source granularity and surface strata that need more open-source research.

Recent empirical work — Pew Research Center 2025, the 2024 Australian Instagram-nutrition study (International Journal of Behavioral Nutrition and Physical Activity), and the 2025 Nickel study (University of Sydney School of Public Health) — quantifies the influencer error rate this pipeline is built to survive and motivates commercial-interest tracking in the practitioner registry. See section three for the full empirical context; these findings validate the existing schema-gate-and-council architecture rather than altering it.

The implementation runner is Hermes Agent (decision 2026-05-22), configured for stateless task execution per `hermes-setup.md`. The durable commitments independent of the runner are the data contracts, standalone `metabolicum-agentic-research` Supabase project, file-system stage boundaries, research target envelope-fact boundary, and controlled export/import to `metasync`. Model routing remains provider-agnostic: Gemini handles YouTube and long-context review, `twitter-mcp` plus SearXNG handle X/Twitter maintenance and discovery for the first implementation, local or task-appropriate models may handle suitable extraction workloads, and diverse model families can serve as parallel cross-validators. The legal posture is conservative throughout: line-based factual citation of public research is the safe lane under Feist Publications versus Rural Telephone Service, 499 U.S. 340 (1991); verbatim table reproduction and bulk corpus ingestion are not. The first release goal is five reviewed pilot markers with enough evidence quality to justify controlled handoff to `metasync`.
