# Legal and IP agent

The legal and IP agent is the gating layer between an approved BiomarkerClaim and a row in the research database. It reasons about copyright, terms of service, and licensing — not about medicine. The posture is conservative throughout.

The foundation is Feist Publications, Incorporated versus Rural Telephone Service Company, 499 U.S. 340 (1991). The Supreme Court held that "facts are not copyrightable" and "no author may copyright his ideas or the facts he narrates" (Justia, retrieved 2026-05-13). The Court further held that "Rural's white pages, limited to basic subscriber information and arranged alphabetically, fall short of the mark. … Indeed, were we to hold that Rural's white pages pass muster, it is hard to believe that any collection of facts could fail" (Cornell LII, retrieved 2026-05-13). The translation into our pipeline is straightforward. A numeric fact, such as "TG/HDL below 2 is the conventional optimal threshold," is not copyrightable. A specific verbatim sentence stating that fact is. A compilation table reproducing multiple thresholds in the same selection and arrangement as a textbook may be copyrightable if the textbook clears the "modicum of creativity" bar. The line-versus-table distinction the user discovered in earlier work is correct: we cite facts line by line with attribution, and we do not reproduce tables as compilations.

The European Union Database Directive (96/9/EC) creates a parallel concern. Even non-original databases that involved "substantial investment in obtaining, verifying or presenting the contents" enjoy a fifteen-year sui generis right (European Commission digital strategy page, retrieved 2026-05-13). Per Wikipedia's database-right entry (retrieved 2026-05-13), "A database right is a sui generis property right, comparable to but distinct from copyright, that exists to recognise the investment that is made in compiling a database, even when this does not involve the 'creative' aspect that is reflected by copyright." Translation: even where Feist allows US extraction, the same table published by an EU entity may be protected against "substantial part" extraction. The legal agent flags EU-published-database sources for higher scrutiny.

The 2025–2026 AI fair-use case law is unsettled but converging on a clear safe lane for our posture. Bartz v. Anthropic (N.D. Cal., June 23 2025, Alsup J.) found training on legally purchased works fair use while holding pirated copies "inherently, irredeemably infringing"; the pirated-library claim later settled. Kadrey v. Meta (N.D. Cal., June 25 2025, Chhabria J.) found fair use on a thin record while explicitly warning that the ruling does not validate Meta's broader practice. Thomson Reuters v. Ross Intelligence (D. Del., Feb 11 2025, Bibas J.) — the first decision rejecting AI-training fair use — is now on Third Circuit appeal (No. 25-8018) and applies only to non-generative AI. NYT v. OpenAI/Microsoft (S.D.N.Y., Stein J.) remains in discovery; the April 4 2025 order let the direct-infringement and trademark-dilution claims proceed. The 2026 publisher class action against Meta (Penguin Random House et al.) reaches academic/educational/textbook markets and bears watching. (All case summaries retrieved 2026-05-13.)

`[JUDGMENT]` Three operational rules flow from this case law. First, we do not train models on copyrighted text. We cite and quote — short, line-level, attributed, fair-use-grounded extractions. Second, no shadow libraries: no LibGen, Z-Library, PiLiMi, or Books3. Third, for papers we use abstract plus metadata always, and full text only from the PMC Open Access Commercial-Use-Allowed subset. This posture survives every ruling above.

For YouTube transcripts, the terms-of-service hierarchy is clear. Tier one is safe: Gemini's native YouTube URL ingestion is Google-sanctioned. Tier two is gray: public auto-generated captions accessed via `youtube-transcript-api`. For fair-use factual quotation with attribution, the risk is low; for republication or training, the risk is high. We only quote. Tier three is avoided: authenticated pages, audio downloads, and access-control bypass.

For podcast transcripts, Podscan and Listen Notes both operate as commercial services with terms that permit downstream use within license bounds. The legal agent records `transcript_source` per claim. Some podcasts assert copyright on their transcripts, which restricts us to short fair-use quotation.

For social media post quotations, short verbatim quotations of public X and Telegram posts for factual analysis and citation are firmly within fair use under the section 107 four-factor test. The legal agent enforces that quotes are at most the platform's character limit, or at most 280 characters for excerpts; that attribution is mandatory; and that bulk reproduction — more than five quotes from the same author in the same table without review — does not occur.

For long-form sources such as podcasts, videos, blogs, books, newsletters, and papers, the quote-length policy is stricter than "whatever fits." The default is the shortest contiguous excerpt needed to support the numeric claim, normally one sentence and preferably under 80 words. Longer excerpts require legal rationale in `legal_reviews.rationale`. The pipeline must not reproduce tables, lists of thresholds, extended transcript passages, or compilations whose selection and arrangement could substitute for the source.

Research target envelope facts are private internal goals and are not legal support for publication. The legal agent treats any attempt to cite, quote, export, or publish an envelope fact as a policy violation. Sanitized envelope facts may guide research convergence internally, but every public or production-facing numeric claim must still stand on approved open-source evidence with its own quote, locator, provenance, and legal review. The source-bearing envelope derivation file is never exposed to agents or export artifacts.

For scraping infrastructure, the posture is to minimize reliance. Apify is used for one specific role only: the Telegram `t.me/s/` public preview surface, where Apify's Telegram actor wraps Telegram's own SEO preview HTML (Apify Telegram scraper documentation, retrieved 2026-05-13). Even there, we treat the output as discovery signal, not as a dataset. Bright Data is avoided — even where courts have sided with scrapers on Computer Fraud and Abuse Act grounds, contract-based theories of breach of platform terms remain a live risk, and our use case does not require Bright Data's scale. Common Crawl is not used as a content source; we may use it for low-stakes practitioner enumeration, such as confirming that a person has a public web presence, but not for ingestion.

The Creative Commons compatibility matrix is summarized as follows, in prose so it cannot be mistaken for a copyrightable compilation table. CC0 and CC BY allow full ingestion with attribution. CC BY-SA allows full ingestion with attribution and share-alike on derivatives. CC BY-ND allows full quotes only, no modification, with attribution. CC BY-NC and its variants (CC BY-NC-SA, CC BY-NC-ND) are non-commercial only; because Metabolicum is commercial, we cannot ingest under these licenses. No-license and custom-license sources default to deny — abstract and metadata only — pending manual review.

A separate lane governs short factual quotation, and it must not be conflated with the license matrix above. The Creative Commons matrix decides whether a source may be ingested wholesale under its license; it does not decide whether a single non-copyrightable fact may be quoted. Per Feist a numeric fact is not copyrightable, and per the section 107 four-factor test a short, attributed, line-level excerpt supporting that fact is fair use regardless of the source's reuse license. The metabolic-optimization paradigm depends entirely on this lane, because named-practitioner web content — blogs, podcast and video pages, public captions — is almost always all-rights-reserved and would otherwise be quarantined wholesale, leaving the paradigm with nothing to publish. The discovery agents therefore stamp such sources with explicit fair-use tags rather than a reuse license: `all_rights_reserved_public_web_page` and `all_rights_reserved_public_web_excerpt` for web surfaces, and `youtube_public_caption_fair_use_quote_only` for public captions. Each means exactly "no reuse license is granted, but the content is publicly visible and a short attributed factual quote is permissible." The legal agent does not auto-quarantine these on license. It records the tag as source metadata, enforces the long-form quote-length policy below, requires attribution, and routes the claim to the legal reviewer for the section 107 determination, recording the basis in `legal_reviews.applicable_rules` as `fair_use_line_quotation` together with `feist_facts_not_copyrightable`. This lane never relaxes the hard rejections: shadow-library sources, non-commercial CC licenses, envelope facts, empty quotes, and table or compilation reproduction still reject, and genuinely no-license or custom-license sources — those carrying no fair-use tag at all — still default to deny pending manual review. The distinction is deliberate: the license matrix gates copying a work; the fair-use lane gates quoting a fact.

HIPAA compliance is structurally guaranteed rather than policy-enforced. The `metasync` user-facing product handles user-uploaded lab data, which is HIPAA-relevant. The agentic research pipeline ingests only public content and runs inside the standalone `metabolicum-agentic-research` project, with its own Supabase project and no credentials for the `metasync` production database. No user protected health information ever touches the agentic research pipeline. The project boundary is the strongest cleanliness guarantee — not policy, architecture.

Public-person data still needs a takedown path. If a named practitioner or organization requests removal, the registry entry is archived rather than hard-deleted, affected `biomarker_claims` are moved back to `approval_status='quarantined'` with a `takedown_requested` rejection code, and source quotations are removed from future exports unless a legal reviewer explicitly preserves them for a defensible citation reason. The audit trail records the request, action taken, and reviewer identity. This is not a HIPAA issue; it is governance for public professional data and jurisdictional privacy risk.

The legal agent's output is a `LegalReview` record, persisted in `legal_reviews`. This is contract notation, not a commitment to a specific runtime framework:

```python
class LegalReview:
    biomarker_claim_id: uuid.UUID
    decision: Literal["approve", "approve_with_modification", "quarantine", "reject"]
    rationale: str
    applicable_rules: list[str]
    quote_length_check: bool
    license_check: bool
    tos_check: bool
    feist_compilation_risk: Literal["none", "low", "medium", "high"]
    reviewed_at: datetime
    reviewer_model: str
```

Only decisions of "approve" or "approve_with_modification" can set `biomarker_claims.approval_status = 'approved'`. `approve_with_modification` is limited to non-substantive safety edits: quote truncation, attribution correction, source locator correction, license note addition, or removal of surplus copied text. It must not change the numeric claim, marker, direction, population qualifier, paradigm, evidence grade, or meaning. A "quarantine" decision creates or updates a `quarantine` row with `rejection_stage: 'legal'` and the relevant rule codes. A "reject" decision does the same with `review_outcome: 'rejected_permanently'`. Legal review therefore gates export, not extraction.
